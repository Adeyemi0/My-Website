Project Overview

A retail business came to me in crisis: revenue had collapsed by nearly 90% over 12 months, yet leadership couldn't pinpoint why. The raw data existed — sales figures, transaction logs, customer records — but nobody had connected the dots into a clear picture.

My task was to build a comprehensive Power BI dashboard that would not only surface what was happening, but explain why it was happening and what to do about it. This project demonstrates exactly what I do: transform messy business data into a clear, actionable story.

Key finding: The revenue collapse was almost entirely driven by an 85% drop in repeat customer retention. Customers were buying once and never returning — while the business kept spending on acquisition. Fixing the leak required finding it first.

Business Problem

The business was in firefighting mode. Leadership had tried increasing ad spend, launching promotions, and onboarding new product lines — yet revenue kept declining. Without a clear diagnosis, every intervention was a guess.

The data held answers to critical questions the team couldn't answer on their own:

  • Was the decline driven by fewer new customers, or fewer returning ones?
  • Which product categories were losing traction — and which were still healthy?
  • Was there a geographic concentration to the losses?
  • At what point in the customer journey were people disengaging?
  • Were seasonal patterns masking or amplifying the underlying trend?

The challenge wasn't purely technical — it was diagnostic. Building the right dashboard meant first understanding which questions, if answered, would actually change what the business decided to do next.

Dataset

The analysis was built on the client's historical transactional and customer records, structured across several related tables and connected into a star schema model:

  • Sales transactions — order dates, quantities, product IDs, and associated store IDs
  • Customer records — anonymized demographic data, acquisition dates, and geographic location
  • Product catalog — categories, subcategories, unit prices, and margins
  • Store data — location, opening date, and channel type (online vs. physical)
  • Calendar table — built in DAX to enable time intelligence across all report pages

The datasets required significant cleaning in Power Query before modeling: date formats were inconsistent across stores, several customer IDs had duplicates from system migrations, and the product hierarchy needed rebuilding from flat CSV exports.

Methodology

The project followed a structured diagnostic workflow — from raw data to root cause:

1

Data Profiling & Quality Assessment

Column profiling in Power Query revealed missing values, duplicate records, and date inconsistencies. All issues were documented and resolved before any modeling began to ensure measure accuracy.

2

Data Modeling & Relationship Design

A star schema was built with the sales fact table at the center, linked to customer, product, store, and date dimensions. This model powers fast, reliable DAX measures across all report pages.

3

DAX Measure Development

Core business metrics were defined as reusable DAX measures: total revenue, YoY growth, customer retention rate, cohort repeat purchase rate, average order value, and customer lifetime value proxies.

4

Retention Cohort Analysis

Customers were segmented by acquisition month and tracked across subsequent periods. This revealed that recent cohorts had dramatically lower return rates — the core finding that explained the revenue collapse.

5

Dashboard Design & Storytelling

Visuals were arranged to walk stakeholders through a narrative: overall trend → category breakdown → customer behaviour → root cause → recommendations. The dashboard answers questions — it doesn't just display data.

Key Insights

The analysis surfaced four findings that directly shaped the recovery strategy:

Retention Was the Real Problem

An 85% drop in repeat customer rate was the primary driver — not acquisition. The business was refilling a leaking bucket instead of fixing the leak.

Category Performance Was Uneven

Two product categories were still growing while others collapsed. Resources hadn't been reallocated to reflect this — an immediate optimization opportunity.

Cohort Decay Accelerated Post-2023

Pre-2023 customers showed healthy return rates. Post-2023 cohorts showed near-zero repeat purchases — pointing to a product, fulfilment, or experience change.

Geographic Losses Were Concentrated

Three regions accounted for over 70% of the revenue decline. Targeted regional interventions could stabilize the business faster than a broad approach.

Business Impact & Recommendations

The dashboard shifted the conversation from "why is revenue down?" to "here's exactly what to fix and in what order." Three tiers of recommendations were delivered:

Immediate Actions (0–30 days)

  • Launch a win-back campaign targeting 2023–2024 cohorts who purchased once and never returned
  • Investigate what changed in the post-purchase experience after mid-2023 — delivery times, product quality, or communication
  • Reallocate marketing budget toward the two still-growing product categories

Medium-Term (30–90 days)

  • Implement a post-purchase retention sequence (email + SMS) triggered at 14, 30, and 60-day intervals
  • Build repeat purchase rate as a weekly leadership KPI to track recovery progress
  • Concentrate regional sales efforts on the three highest-decline geographies

Structural Changes

  • Shift CAC measurement to include LTV projections — current calculations were ignoring repeat purchase value entirely
  • Set up automated cohort monitoring in Power BI for ongoing visibility without manual reporting

Why this matters: Increasing acquisition spend while retention was broken would have accelerated the decline, not reversed it. Data made this visible in a way that gut feel couldn't — and fast enough to actually act on it.
