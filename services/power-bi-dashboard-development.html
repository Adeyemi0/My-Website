Common Questions

How long does a Power BI dashboard project take?
Most single-dashboard projects take 1–3 weeks depending on data complexity and the number of pages. Multi-dashboard projects or those requiring significant data cleaning take 3–6 weeks. I'll give you a specific timeline after the discovery call when I can see your actual data.
What data sources can you connect to Power BI?
Power BI connects to hundreds of sources. The most common ones I work with are: Excel and CSV files, SQL databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL, SQL Server), Shopify, Google Analytics, Google Sheets, Salesforce, HubSpot, and REST APIs. If you're using something else, just ask — it's almost certainly supported.
Do I need a Power BI Pro license?
To share dashboards with others in your organization, yes — Power BI Pro costs $10/user/month. For a personal dashboard only you will use, the free version is sufficient. I'll advise you on the right setup for your situation during our discovery call.
Will I be able to maintain the dashboard after you deliver it?
Yes — that's a core part of how I work. Every project includes documentation explaining how the data model works, what each DAX measure does, and how to make common updates. I build so that you're self-sufficient, not dependent on me for every change.
How much does a Power BI dashboard project cost?
Pricing depends on data complexity, number of pages, and the level of data modeling required. Simple single-source dashboards start from $500. Multi-source dashboards with complex data modeling and DAX are typically $1,000–$3,000. I'll give you a fixed-price quote after the discovery call — no hourly billing surprises.
