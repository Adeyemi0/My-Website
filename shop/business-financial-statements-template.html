The Core Outputs

The Three Financial Statements Every Business Needs

These are not just nice-to-have reports. They are the language of business finance. Banks require them for loans. Investors require them before funding. Tax authorities require them for compliance. Here is what each one does and why it matters.

Income Statement (Profit & Loss)

Sheet: Income_Statement

Shows everything your business earned and everything it spent over a period of time — and whether you made a profit or a loss. This is the statement most business owners look at first when they want to understand performance.

  • Total revenue broken down by category (Product Sales, Services, Consulting, Tips)
  • Cost of Goods Sold and Gross Profit calculation
  • All operating expenses categorized and totalled
  • Operating Income, interest, tax, and final Net Income
  • View year-to-date or any custom period

Balance Sheet

Sheet: Balance_Sheet

A snapshot of what your business owns, what it owes, and what's left over for the owner. Updated automatically to reflect your current assets, liabilities from the Debt Register, and equipment values from the Asset Register.

  • Current assets: cash, receivables, inventory, prepaid expenses
  • Fixed assets: equipment net book value after depreciation
  • Current and long-term liabilities from Debt Register
  • Equity section with retained earnings
  • Auto-validates that Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Cash Flow Statement

Sheet: Cash_Flow

Shows the actual movement of cash in and out of the business — separate from profit. A business can be profitable on paper and still run out of cash. The Cash Flow Statement is what tells you whether you actually have the money to operate.

  • Cash from Operating Activities — net income adjusted for non-cash items
  • Cash from Investing Activities — equipment purchases and disposals
  • Cash from Financing Activities — loan proceeds and repayments
  • Beginning and ending cash balance reconciliation
  • Cash runway analysis on the Dashboard

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need accounting knowledge to use this template?

No. You only enter your transactions — date, type, category, and amount. The system generates all three financial statements automatically. A full How-To-Use guide is built into the file and walks you through every step.

Does it work for Nigerian businesses?

Yes. The template is configured for NGN (₦) by default and supports 8 currencies: USD, EUR, GBP, NGN, CAD, AUD, JPY, and CNY. Multi-currency transactions are automatically converted to your base currency using exchange rates you set, making it ideal for import/export and businesses that receive foreign payments.

What version of Excel do I need?

Microsoft Excel 2016 or later, including Excel 365. The template uses standard Excel formulas (SUMIFS, VLOOKUP, IF). It is not designed for Google Sheets due to some formula dependencies that behave differently outside Excel.

Can I use it to apply for a bank loan?

Yes. The template generates the three statements banks and lenders require: Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement. The output is professionally formatted and clearly labelled with your company name. Most Nigerian commercial banks and microfinance institutions accept these standard statement formats.

Can I track loans and equipment depreciation?

Yes. The Debt Register tracks all loans with automatic monthly payment calculations, outstanding balances, and overdue alerts. The Equipment Register tracks all assets with straight-line depreciation calculated automatically by useful life. Both feed directly into the Balance Sheet.
